Hi Nanna


Hi Nanna (2023) is a Telugu-language drama film directed by debutant Shouryuv, starring Nani and Mrunal Thakur in lead roles, with Kiara Khanna portraying their daughter Mahi. The film centers on a single father, Viraj, who narrates the story of his missing wife to his daughter, exploring themes of love, parenthood, and resilience against a backdrop of scenic locations like Coonoor and Goa.
While the film received mixed reviews, it is generally praised for its emotional depth, strong performances—particularly from Nani and Mrunal Thakur—and its visually stunning cinematography.

  • The film's narrative follows Viraj, a celebrated photographer in Mumbai, as he navigates single parenthood after his wife Varsha (Mrunal Thakur) goes missing, while their daughter Mahi battles a life-threatening lung condition.
  • Critics highlight the powerful emotional core, with the climax being especially impactful and praised for its writing and performances, elevating the film despite its slow pacing and predictable elements.
  • Nani's performance is lauded for its depth, particularly in the final act, where he delivers a touching portrayal of a father's love and grief.
  • Mrunal Thakur's role as Yashna, a character tied to Viraj's past, is described as the heart and soul of the film, with her screen presence and beauty noted as captivating.
  • The film's technical aspects, including Hesham Abdul Wahab's background score, Sanu John Varghese's cinematography, and Praveen Anthony's editing, are widely appreciated for enhancing the emotional narrative.
  • However, some reviews criticize the film for its slow pacing, predictable romantic storyline, underdeveloped supporting characters, and the inclusion of certain sequences—like the "Odiyamma" song—that disrupt the narrative flow.
  • The film's structure is seen as strong in the first half, with a compelling setup and an effective interval twist, but the second half is considered to drag, with repetitive scenes and a lack of fresh developments.
  • Despite its flaws, Hi Nanna is recommended for audiences seeking a heartfelt, family-oriented drama, with its emotional resonance and performances making it a worthwhile watch.
